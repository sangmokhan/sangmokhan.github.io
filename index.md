## Biography

I am an M.S. student at the [Department of Mechanical and Process Engineering](https://mavt.ethz.ch), ETH Zürich. Previously, I obtained a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering at the [Department of Mechanical Engineering](https://mechanical.eng.unimelb.edu.au), The University of Melbourne.

I am interested in interdisciplinary research centered around fluid mechanics, including wall-bounded turbulence, particle-laden flows, and multiphase flows, using direct numerical simulation, large eddy simulation, or Reynolds-averaged Navier-Stokes equations. By developing numerical tools, I aim to distill the chaotic and complex nature of turbulent flows into simple yet fundamental mechanisms that yield physical insights into fluid flows in various engineering contexts.

Outside fluid mechanics, I am also interested in statistics, especially in probability density estimation. I worked with [Daniel Meyer](https://ifd.ethz.ch/people/person-detail.OTM2MDU=.TGlzdC80NzkwLDEwMTQwMjU1NDg=.html) at the [Institute of Fluid Dynamics](https://ifd.ethz.ch), ETH Zürich, on developing a smooth and adaptive density estimator in uni- and bivariate probability spaces based on the distribution element tree method.

Before joining ETH Zürich, I worked as a Research Intern at the [Turbulence Lab](https://euler.yonsei.ac.kr), Yonsei University, working on numerical analysis of [lid-driven cavity flow](https://github.com/sangmokhan/Lid_driven_cavity_flow) using the Crank-Nicolson/Adams-Bashforth scheme. Before this, I also worked as a Visiting Student at the [Fluid & Interface Laboratory](https://hyoungsookimm.wixsite.com/filkaist), Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology, on embedded 3D printing of liquid metal within viscoplastic matrices.

After graduating from The University of Melbourne, I worked as a Vacation Student at the [Commonwealth Scientific and Industrial Research Organisation](https://www.csiro.au), where I was jointly advised by [Peter Witt](https://people.csiro.au/W/P/Peter-Witt.aspx) and [Yuqing Feng](https://people.csiro.au/F/Y/Yuqing-Feng.aspx). My project focused on developing a CFD model of slag flow on a spinning disk to predict its phase change on the disk surface and flow properties at the disk edge. Subsequently, I was apppointed as a Research Assistant in the same group, working on developing MATLAB code to compute flow properties of gas flow in a Laval nozzle.

During my undergraduate studies, I worked with [Agisilaos Kourmatzis](https://www.sydney.edu.au/engineering/about/our-people/academic-staff/agisilaos-kourmatzis.html) at the [School of Aerospace, Mechanical and Mechatronic Engineering](https://www.sydney.edu.au/engineering/schools/school-of-aerospace-mechanical-and-mechatronic-engineering.html), The University of Sydney, on numerical simulation of intransal drug delivery in laminar and turbulent inhalation flows.
